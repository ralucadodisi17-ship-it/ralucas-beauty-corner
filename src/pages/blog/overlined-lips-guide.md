The overlined lip has a reputation problem. Say it out loud and people immediately picture badly blended liner bleeding into fine lines, or a shape so exaggerated it reads as costume. But that's not what the 90s overline was — and it's not what it needs to be now.

## The original technique

In the 90s, overlining was subtle. The goal wasn't to dramatically reshape the lip — it was to add fullness and create a defined, almost doll-like pout. Liner went just a millimetre outside the natural lip line, following the shape faithfully rather than reinventing it.

The product mattered too. 90s lip liners were soft, almost creamy, and came in colours that matched the lipstick exactly — or in universally flattering nudes that worked with everything.

## The cool-toned palette connection

Here's what made it distinctly 90s: the liner was often a shade *darker* than the lipstick. Dark taupe liner, pale frosted lipstick inside. Cool brown liner, icy mauve centre. That contrast is what gives it the era's characteristic look.

> The secret is choosing a liner that's cool-toned and muted — nothing warm, nothing too brown, nothing too pink.

Shades to look for: dusty mauve, cool taupe, muted plum. Avoid anything with orange or peach in it — that immediately reads as a different decade entirely.

## Step by step

1. Start with moisturised, exfoliated lips — liner needs a smooth surface
2. Choose a cool-toned liner one to two shades deeper than your lipstick
3. Follow your natural lip shape, just barely outside the line — a hair's width
4. Fill in the entire lip with liner before applying lipstick (this makes the colour last)
5. Apply your lipstick, focusing on the centre for a slightly ombré effect
6. Optional: a tiny dab of frosted gloss in the centre only

## The key to keeping it modern

Don't overdo the contrast. The darker-liner-lighter-centre trick works beautifully when the difference is subtle. If your liner is three shades darker than your lipstick, it reads retro in the wrong way. Think of it as dimension, not drama.

And keep everything else minimal. The overlined lip is the moment — it doesn't need competing eyes or cheeks. Soft mascara, cool-toned blush, done.
