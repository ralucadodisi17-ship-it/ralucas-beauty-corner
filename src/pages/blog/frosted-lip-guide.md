If there's one makeup look that instantly transports me back to flipping through teen magazines, it's the frosted lip. That icy, pearlescent sheen — somewhere between a nude and a shimmer — was *everywhere* in the 90s. And honestly? It deserved to be.

## What made the frosted lip so iconic

The 90s were obsessed with contradiction: matte skin but glossy lips, dark liner with pale centres, grunge aesthetics with full glam. The frosted lip sat right in the middle of that tension. It was bold without being vampy, glamorous without being red-carpet.

Think Drew Barrymore, Jennifer Aniston, TLC. Think *every* lip product at your local drugstore coming in shades called "Iced Mocha" or "Frost Berry."

## How to wear it now

The key difference between then and now is formula. 90s frosts were often chalky — they sat on top of the lip rather than melting into it. Modern takes are sheerer, more dimensional.

> Start with a cool-toned nude base — something with a faint pink or mauve undertone. Then press a pearlescent topper over the centre of the lip only.

Products to look for:
- A satin lipstick in dusty rose or cool beige as your base
- A separate shimmer topper (or a highlighter pencil works brilliantly)
- Clear gloss with silver micro-shimmer for the full 90s effect

## The liner question

Overlined or not? In the 90s, yes — always. The dark liner with pale frosted centre was basically the lip look of the decade. Today you can go either way. If you want full nostalgia, line slightly outside your natural lip with a cool taupe pencil. If you want a more modern take, skip the liner entirely and let the shimmer do the work.

## Cool tones are key

This is non-negotiable. A warm coral with pearl = very 2000s. A cool mauve, icy pink, or nude with lavender undertones = distinctly, beautifully 90s. When in doubt, go cooler than you think you need to.

The frosted lip isn't a costume. It's a whole mood — luminous, a little otherworldly, and completely timeless.
